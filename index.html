Sponsors

We are a link between you (the sponsor) and the association/team. We mediate the contact between the sponsor and the association/team. We help with all administration (agreement, invoicing, payment to the team/association, re-reporting, etc.) and make it easier for the sponsor and the team.

We are very open and transparent. Obviously, like all other companies, we also make money from this, but unlike most companies, we show openly our merit. For us, Sponsorportalen is more than a company and money, read more about us here

In Practical Terms, It Works Like This

Phase One
Step #1
Choose Country
You choose in which part of the country you want to sponsor an association
Step #2
Choose Sport
You choose which sport you want to sponsor
Step #3
Choose Boys or Girls
You choose whether you want to sponsor boys or girls
Step #4
Choose Association
You choose whether you want to sponsor an association/team in a socially vulnerable area
Step #5
Choose Packages
You choose one of our fixed sponsorship packages (bronze, silver, and gold)
Step #6
Choose Voluntary
You choose how often you want to sponsor (voluntary and non-binding, we contact you before each sponsorship assignment)
Step #7
Fill the Form
You fill in your and the company's details CLEAR! (NOTE! This is the first step, no invoice will be sent at this stage!)
Phase One
Step #1
Contact with associations
We contact associations/teams based on your wishes. If you chose "doesn't matter" then we select an association/team from our database.
Step #2
Establish with association
That they can accept the sponsorship and want to complete the agreement depending on which sponsor package it is.
Step #3
Informing you
We inform you/the sponsor that we match your requests with a suitable association/team, send information about the association/team and asks if you are satisfied with the choice and want to complete the agreement.
Step #4
If not satisfied
If no, we ask for further requests and continue looking until you feel satisfied with the choice.
Step #5
If satisfied
If yes, we draw up a sponsorship agreement between the sponsor and the association which is sent to both signatures through electronic service.
Step #6
Invoice
When the agreement is written, we send an invoice to sponsorship.
Step #7
Payment
When the invoice is paid, we send the sponsorship to the team/association.
Step #8
Expense report
The team/association has an obligation to report within 6 months that the sponsorship has been spent according to the agreement (invoices, receipts, pictures) which we report to the sponsor.

Teams

SponsorPortalen is a link between companies/sponsors, who want to make one effort for children and youth sports through sponsorship, and the association/team.

SponsorPortalen is only intended for associations/teams within children's and youth sports.

The association/team can completely free of charge search current and active sponsor applications on the Sponsorportalen and register interest.

Humanitarian fund

The sponsor portal has its own humanitarian fund with the goal of helping children's athletes. Sponsorportalen allocates 2% of each invoice to the fund and sponsors can contribute any amount they wish
The money can be applied for
Membership
To pay membership and training fees to the association
Equipment
To buy the necessary equipment (shoes, training clothes)
Camp or Trips
Paying for training camp or cup trips
Who Can Apply?
Poor finance
Parents/Guardians of children/youths of any age with poor finances who cannot afford the above.
By filling the form
The application is made through the form here. So that the money from the Humanitarian Fund goes to those who really need it most and is not misused need to supplement your application with the necessary certificates. All personal data is handled in accordance with the GDPR.

About Us
The beginning

The boy was 6 years old, the first football school was completed and a letter arrived from the local football association. "If you want your children to continue practicing football, it is necessary for parents to stand up as coaches and team managers." The association assists with materials, advice, and training. Why not, we thought, we would still be driving the boy to train, sit and wait for him until training is finished. Then you might as well bring some balls and cones and organize some games and exercises. We'll see if the boy is even interested. 10 years later, you looked back and realized that you spent thousands and thousands of hours as father/coach/team manager/treasurer/administrator/driver on a bunch of lovely guys who stayed together for 10 years through thick and thin, traveled the country on cups and matches, fought many fights on the football field but also off the football field.

When you as an outsider think of a youth team, you think of training and matches. Well, most of it revolves around training and matches. But association life is so much more. Each team is like a small company with a division of tasks, finances, and a lot of hard work. Membership and training fees must be collected from the association because without a functioning association there are no teams. If you want to have something fun besides training and playing local matches against the same team over and over again, you have to draw money. Not everything can be paid out of pocket. Everything costs money. Not everyone can afford it. We had a philosophy: EVERYONE should join, regardless of background, status, and finances! That's why we sold lottery tickets and everything possible, distributed newspapers, stood as hosts and ball callers at Allsvenskan matches, picked up trash.

But it was worth it!

To see happy guys go on the cup and experience things most people could never afford was an incredible satisfaction for all the hard work, it was worth every drop of sweat, every hour of work of thousands of working hours, all frozen toes, too little sleep. The team was a lovely mix of different nationalities. Most of the boys lived in socially disadvantaged areas, most could barely afford soccer shoes, and most of them never got a ride to training and matches... But the vast majority stayed together for 10 years, during the "worst" youth period, trained 4-5 times a week, and played 1-2 games a week. Their life was football and there was no time for nonsense.

No one became a professional soccer player even though many were very talented but the biggest gain for all the boys, all the parents, all the families, and the whole society was that no one got in contact with crime. Because they had an alternative! But we were lucky! My coaching colleagues and I had good contacts! We knew people who knew people. We could fix quests, earn money, find sponsors. But not everyone is lucky. They need guidance from those who understand. That's how SponsorPortalen was born, to help teams and kids cherish some of their best years in a proper way.
